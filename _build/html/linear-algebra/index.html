Vectors¶

A vector in Rn\R^nRn is an ordered list of nnn real numbers. We write column vectors as u=(u1,…,un)\vec{u} = (u_1, \dots, u_n)u=(u1​,…,un​) with each ui∈Ru_i \in \Rui​∈R.

The dot product of u,v∈Rn\vec{u}, \vec{v} \in \R^nu,v∈Rn is the scalar

u⋅v=∑i=1nui vi.\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} u_i\, v_i .u⋅v=i=1∑n​ui​vi​.
(1)

For a concrete computation, take u=(1,2,3)\vec{u} = (1, 2, 3)u=(1,2,3) and v=(4,5,6)\vec{v} = (4, 5, 6)v=(4,5,6). Then by equation (1),

u⋅v=1⋅4+2⋅5+3⋅6=32.\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 1\cdot 4 + 2\cdot 5 + 3\cdot 6 = 32 .u⋅v=1⋅4+2⋅5+3⋅6=32.
(2)

In Lean, a vector is a function Fin n → ℝ, and ![…] is notation for one. The dot product and the matrix operations below follow the spellings used in Mathlib 1:[1]

Matrices¶

An m×nm \times nm×n matrix A∈Rm×nA \in \R^{m \times n}A∈Rm×n is a rectangular array of scalars. For example,

A=[1234]∈R2×2.A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \in \R^{2 \times 2}.A=[13​24​]∈R2×2.
(3)

The matrix–vector product AxA\vec{x}Ax takes a vector x∈Rn\vec{x} \in \R^nx∈Rn to a vector in Rm\R^mRm. With x=(5,6)\vec{x} = (5, 6)x=(5,6):

Linear maps¶

A linear map T:Rn→RmT : \R^n \to \R^mT:Rn→Rm is a function satisfying T(αu+v)=α T(u)+T(v)T(\alpha\vec{u} + \vec{v}) = \alpha\,T(\vec{u}) + T(\vec{v})T(αu+v)=αT(u)+T(v) for all scalars α\alphaα and vectors u,v\vec{u}, \vec{v}u,v. Every such map is represented by a matrix, and matrix multiplication corresponds to composition of the maps.
